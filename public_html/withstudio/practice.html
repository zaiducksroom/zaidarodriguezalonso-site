Practice

WITH studio is a small design practice exploring how humans inhabit digital spaces.

The studio investigates how perception, interaction and atmosphere shape the experience of digital environments.

Designing digital spaces

Digital experiences are often treated as interfaces. However, they can also function as spaces.

People move through them, observe their structure and gradually learn how to orient themselves. Designing these environments means thinking about perception, navigation and atmosphere.

Understanding perception

People do not experience digital systems only through actions. They experience them through perception.

Digital environments are spaces that people learn to inhabit.

Light, rhythm, movement and interaction influence how a digital environment is understood.

Inviting exploration

Not every digital system needs to guide the user through a predefined path.

Some environments benefit from curiosity and slow exploration, allowing people to discover connections and meanings over time.

Designing cultural environments

Digital systems can also function as cultural spaces.

Museums, exhibitions and interactive environments can become places where people explore ideas, narratives and atmospheres.

Areas of work
